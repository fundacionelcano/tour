In the Tomb T1 (Cal 780 to 970 A.D.) eight individuals were buried. The main one is an adult of indeterminate sex. This adult was accompanied by a twelve-year-old child of high status, and six individuals of low status: a child, two adult women, an adult male and two adults of indeterminate sex.

1. Bracelet. This was decorated with two anthropomorphic crocodiles that, bound by a belt, walk upright in the opposite direction. The pair has the same design. Iconographically it is a variant of a central theme in Cocle art: the Mythic Double Crocodile.

2. Zoomorphic vessel. This represents a female monkey with its two offspring.

3. Spherical beads. These were woven in a band forming small clusters.

4‑5. Pectorals. Their designs are two representations of a hybrid squid and bird: the Flying Squid. It has elements of supernaturality such as the forked tongue (which at the same time figures to be a pair of arms) and the belt (which is also the two fins unfolded). If we turn the figure 180º we discover a pair of birds hidden in its head.

6. Bracelet of Bells (39 units). The bells were used in religious ceremonies, rites and dances, and their use was restricted to the ruling class.

7. Belt. This is composed of 63 spherical beads. It is possible that, at the time, each of them had a core of wood. These would give them weight and consistency. Without them they are extremely fragile and easily deformed.

8. Tubular beads (384 units). These were sewn to two greaves. Greaves are garments used to dress and protect the legs.

9. Zoomorphic jar. This was modeled in the shape of a bird carrying a vessel (offering) on its head. It is the metaphorical representation of one of the officiants of the funerary ritual. It was placed inside the tomb one year after the burial.

10‑11. Axes. The handle on the axes was placed parallel to the edge.

12. Adze. Adzes were used for roughing wood and probably also as hoes for weeding. The handle, in this type of artifact, was placed perpendicular to the edge.

13‑17. Honing tools. These tools were used to brighten or polish a ceramic or metal object.

18. Figurative Pendant. This represents a pelican with its head turned and resting on its back. It was part of a necklace made up of feline canines.
