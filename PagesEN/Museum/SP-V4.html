The tomb T6 (Cal 775 to 980 AD) was not found in its entirety. It was partly destroyed when the tombs T1 and T2 were built. Only the bones of the legs of two adult individuals of indeterminate sex and a skull were preserved. All of the artifacts that can be seen in this showcase were found in one corner of the tomb.

1. Square bowl. This was decorated with geometric motifs "YC".

2-3. Semi-spherical bowls. These were decorated with crab designs.

4. Pectoral. This displays a Mythical Double Crocodile, a bicephalous crocodile. Its body is an oblong rectangle from which its two heads emerge opposite of each other.

5-6. Bracelets. These are a set of trunk-conical artifacts for the arms.

7. Figurative pendant. This represents a frog or toad.

8. Figurative pendant. This represents a bird of prey.

9. Set of circular plates. These have holes to be sewn or hung.

10. Spool earmuffs. These were decorated with geometric and biomorphic motifs.

11. Figurative pendant. This represents a possum putting an object in its mouth. It has two ridges and a belt, two elements that express supernaturality.

12. Figurative pendant. This displays a schematic crocodile body with human head. This one, that by a defect in the process of smelting has deformed the jaw, carries facial paintings and a headdress that combines elements of tradition Quimbaya and Zenú.

13. Bell with cylindrical handle.

14. Bell-pendant. This represents an owl with folded wings. In some pre-Hispanic societies this nocturnal predatory and ferocious bird of prey plays the role of messenger and servant of the gods of the underworld.

15. Figurative pendant. This represents a hummingbird in static flight. The artist takes advantage of the physical characteristics of the animal to present its body as a flower.

16. Bell pendant. This represents the head of a peccary with an open mouth. In the mouth there is a small sphere.

17. Perforated stone. This is one of the two elements that made up a figurative spider-shaped pendant, in which the emerald represented the animal's abdomen.

18. Figure. This artifact depicts the sacrificial offering of an infant by an officiant carrying a monkey on his shoulders.
